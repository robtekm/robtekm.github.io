About Me

I hold a bachelor’s degree in Physics Education from the Federal Institute of São Paulo and am currently a master’s student at the Institute of Physics, University of São Paulo. My research focuses on luminescence mechanisms in minerals, with applications in radiation dosimetry and dating. My master’s thesis investigates phototransfer and thermally assisted mechanisms in potassium feldspars.

Recent Publications

Optically stimulated luminescence of amazonite using linear modulated technique

Journal of Luminescence, 2025 — https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jlumin.2025.121610

Investigation of fundamental LM-OSL properties in amazonite K-feldspar using a Localized Transition Model (LTM) considering ground and excited state tunneling.

Structural, optical and thermoluminescence properties of amazonite

Journal for Radiation Physics and Chemistry, 2024 — https://doi.org/10.1016/j.radphyschem.2024.111947

Investigation of basic structural properties using X-ray diffraction (XRD), including Al/Si order–disorder and aluminum occupancy at tetrahedral sites, complemented by X-ray fluorescence (XRF), optical and vibrational spectroscopies, and thermoluminescence analysis under varying heating rates, doses, pre-heating conditions, and fading effects.

Thermoluminescence of rose quartz from Minas Gerais, Brazil

Journal for Radiation Physics and Chemistry, 2023 — https://doi.org/10.1016/j.radphyschem.2023.110960

Investigation basic structural properties via XRD, XRF and thermoluminescence properties as the variable heating, dose, pre-heating, fading dependence of TL glow curve.
