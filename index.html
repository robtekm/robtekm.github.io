About Me

I am an experimental physicist with a background in Physics Education and currently pursuing a Master`s degree at the Institute of Physics, University of São Paulo (USP). My work focuses on luminescence mechanisms in minerals, with applications in radiation dosimetry and dating. My master's research investigates phototransfer and thermally assisted luminescence processes in potassium feldspars, combining experimental spectroscopy and kinetic modeling.

Recent Publications

Optically stimulated luminescence of amazonite using linear modulated technique

Martins, R. T. E. K., Yoshimura, E. M., Trindade, N. M.

Journal of Luminescence, 2025 — https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jlumin.2025.121610

Investigation of fundamental LM-OSL properties in amazonite K-feldspar using a Localized Transition Model (LTM), considering ground- and excited-state tunneling processes.

Structural, optical and thermoluminescence properties of amazonite

Martins, R. T. E. K., Künzel, R., Ulsen, C., Yoshimura, E. M., Trindade, N. M.

Journal of Radiation Physics and Chemistry, 2024 — https://doi.org/10.1016/j.radphyschem.2024.111947

Investigation of structural properties using X-ray diffraction (XRD), including Al/Si order–disorder and aluminum occupancy at tetrahedral sites, complemented by XRF, optical and vibrational spectroscopies, and thermoluminescence analysis under varying experimental conditions.

Thermoluminescence of rose quartz from Minas Gerais, Brazil

Martins, R. T. E. K., Ferreira, I. A., Silva, A. O., Nunes, M. C. S., Ulsen, C., Künzel, R., Souza, M. M., Chithambo, M. L., Yoshimura, E. M., Trindade, N. M.

Journal of Radiation Physics and Chemistry, 2023 — https://doi.org/10.1016/j.radphyschem.2023.110960

Study of the structural, chemical, and thermoluminescence properties of rose quartz from Minas Gerais, including a comparative analysis of kinetic parameters obtained using multiple TL analysis methods.
